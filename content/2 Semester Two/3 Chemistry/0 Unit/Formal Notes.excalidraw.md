Density
Density is defined as the ratio of mass to volume of a sample. ( How heavy is it for it's size ? ) ( D = m/v ) ( Density = mass / volume ) 
Substances float when they are less dense than the substance they are in. 
Using density values, Is water (1.00 g/ml)) more or less dense than vegetable oil (0.85 g/ml)?  Water is more dense than vegetable oil 
Look at the density values to compare the substances.  The larger the density, the more dense!
Density does vary with temperature. 
Why?
Most substances will expand when heated, increasing the volume and decreasing the density. 
But water is an exception. As water cools, it expands, increasing the volume and decreasing the density... Ice Floats on water
